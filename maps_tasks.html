Maps

Among Us contains multiple different maps players can play on. Below are images of these locations, as well as some defining characteristics:

The SKELD:

The SKELD is the "first" map in the game. It's usually known as the default map, as it's the one players tend to play the most. This is proven by the general architecture of the starting screen, where players seem to be in the "dropship" part of the map. The design is pretty straightforward, featuring all of the game's most famous locations (Electrical, Medbay, Navigation). Lore-wise, the map is thought of as a spaceship, transporting players between MIRA HQ and POLUS.
Some tasks are exclusive to The SKELD. These include "Align Engine Output" and "Empty Chutes".

MIRA HQ:

MIRA HQ is the smallest Among Us map, usually referred to as "the worst" by hardcore players. It features a very futuristic design, where the hallways look extremely clean and polished. MIRA also proves other forms of life exist in the game, as there is a large tree encased in glass. This tree is one of the defining features of the map. Others include the sensors. (see more in Sabotages and Other Interactions)

POLUS:

POLUS is thought of as the final landing destination. It's built like a research station, where players leave the dropship of the SKELD to do their tasks on land. It also seems to be an alien planet that they've landed on, where the ground is purple and the sky is pitch black. The specimen lab in POLUS requires players to inspect things like fossils, as well as sort different objects. There's also a telescope in Medbay which is used to inspect and look for other celestial bodies in space.

The name "Polus" actually comes from another character in Innersloth's earlier game. The character, Polus Petrovich, apparently had a planet named after him - connecting the two universes.

AIRSHIP:

AIRSHIP is the newest map, featuring a red ship flying through the sky. It's currently unclear who owns the ship, why crewmates have access to it, and where it's going. It's massive, giving players the option to spawn on any floor, as well as a specific room. There's nothing much to note about Airship, other than a couple of new tasks. The developers did, however, include a very cool easter egg. The map was based on Innersloth's other game, the fourth episode of the Henry Stickman Collection. The game was titled "Infiltrating the Airship", creating this connection. It even features the same red ship!

Tasks

There are many different tasks in Among Us, making players do many different things.


Common Tasks:

Common tasks are tasks that every single crewmate has. These tasks are like Admin card swipe, POLUS keys, etc. Since Impostors aren't able to do tasks, it's always worth keeping an eye out for anyone faking a common task that you don't have.


Here is a list of some Common Tasks and their locations:

Card Swipe SKELD Admin
Wires SKELD, MIRA, POLUS, AIRSHIP Hallways, Electrical

Sabotages and Other Interactions

Sabotages:

  • Lights - Crewmates lose vision temporarily (until they fix the lights), Impostors are immune
  • Oxygen/O2 - Impostors cut the oxygen for all Crewmates. Impostors win if the Crewmates don't fix the sabotage in 30-45 seconds
  • Reactor - Impostors shut down the reactor. Two Crewmates need to be fixing it at the same time. Impostors win if crewmates don't fix the sabotage in 60 seconds
  • Communications - Crewmates are unable to do tasks until the sabotage is fixed, buying Impostors some time

Impostors are also able to shut doors. These doors remain locked for a brief period, only being openable on POLUS. If a sabotage is currently going off, Crewmates are unable to press the emergency button. The only way to call a meeting would be through reporting a dead body.


Other Interactions:

  • Admin Table - A panel in Admin which lets a player (any role) see the location of all other players. These icons, however, remain anonymous.
  • Vitals - A menu which lets players see the living/death status of other players. If a Crewmate dies, their character will show up as red on this menu.
  • Security Cameras - Gives players access to Security Cameras located all across the map. Players can open up the cams and see anyone who's standing under them.
  • MIRA HQ Sensors - Exclusive to the MIRA HQ map, players can open up the "Sensor Logs" which shows all players who pass a certain location.
